## Understanding P/E Ratios

### What P/E Represents

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Definition | Price / Earnings Per Share |
| Meaning | How much investors pay per $1 of earnings |
| Higher P/E | Higher growth expectations |
| Lower P/E | Lower growth or value |

### Sector P/E Calculation

| Method | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Market-Cap Weighted | Larger companies have more influence |
| Equal Weighted | Each company equal influence |
| Aggregate | Total market cap / Total earnings |

## Comparing Company to Sector

### Basic Comparison

| Comparison | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| Company P/E < Sector | Trading at discount |
| Company P/E = Sector | Fairly valued |
| Company P/E > Sector | Trading at premium |

### Premium/Discount Calculation

```
Premium/Discount % = (Company P/E - Sector P/E) / Sector P/E Ã— 100%
```

### Example Analysis

If a tech company has P/E of 45 vs. sector P/E of 58.77:
- Discount: (45 - 58.77) / 58.77 = -23.4%
- **Interpretation**: Trading at 23% discount to sector

## Red Flags

### Warning Signs

1. **Extreme P/E** (>100) - Earnings issues or speculation
2. **Negative P/E** - Sector losing money
3. **Rapid expansion** - Valuation stretched
4. **Divergence from earnings growth** - Disconnect

### Data Quality Issues

| Issue | Concern |
|-------|---------|
| Negative earners excluded | May not represent all |
| Outliers | Can skew average |
| Stale data | Earnings may have changed |

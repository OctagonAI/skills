# Interpreting Cash Flow Review Results

## Reading the Output

The octagon-agent returns structured cash flow analysis with source citations:

**Cash Flow Summary:**
- Operating Cash Flow: Amount and trend
- Investing Cash Flow: CapEx and investments
- Financing Cash Flow: Debt and equity activities

**Working Capital:**
- Key changes in current assets/liabilities
- Cash conversion metrics

## Understanding Cash Flow Components

### Operating Cash Flow

| Element | What It Shows |
|---------|---------------|
| Net Income Start | Profitability base |
| D&A Add-back | Non-cash expense |
| Working Capital | Operational efficiency |
| Other Adjustments | Non-cash items |
| Total OCF | Cash from operations |

### Investing Cash Flow

| Element | What It Shows |
|---------|---------------|
| CapEx | Property, equipment investment |
| Acquisitions | M&A spending |
| Investments | Securities activity |
| Dispositions | Asset sales |
| Total ICF | Investment activity |

### Financing Cash Flow

| Element | What It Shows |
|---------|---------------|
| Debt Activity | Borrowing changes |
| Equity Activity | Stock transactions |
| Dividends | Distributions |
| Total FCF | Capital structure changes |

## Evaluating Cash Flow Quality

### High-Quality Cash Flow

| Indicator | Why It Matters |
|-----------|----------------|
| OCF > Net Income | Cash backs earnings |
| Stable OCF growth | Predictable generation |
| Positive FCF | Self-funding capability |
| Low non-cash adjustments | Earnings quality |

### Low-Quality Cash Flow

| Indicator | Concern |
|-----------|---------|
| OCF < Net Income | Earnings not cash-backed |
| Volatile OCF | Unpredictable |
| Negative FCF | Cash consumption |
| Heavy adjustments | Questionable earnings |

### Quality Ratio Analysis

| Ratio | Calculation | Target |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| Cash Conversion | OCF / Net Income | >100% |
| FCF Conversion | FCF / Net Income | >80% |
| OCF / Revenue | OCF / Revenue | Varies by industry |
| CapEx / OCF | CapEx / OCF | <50% for mature |

## Analyzing Working Capital Trends

### Component Analysis

| Component | Increase | Decrease |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| Receivables | Sales growth or collection slowdown | Collections improve |
| Inventory | Building stock or demand weakness | Selling through or shortage |
| Payables | Extended terms or cash conservation | Faster payment |
| Deferred Revenue | Advance collections | Revenue recognition |

### Cash Conversion Cycle

| CCC Trend | Interpretation |
|-----------|----------------|
| Shortening | Improved efficiency |
| Lengthening | Cash tied up longer |
| Negative | Excellent position |
| Volatile | Operational issues |

### Working Capital Efficiency

| Metric | Good | Concern |
|--------|------|---------|
| DSO | Stable or declining | Rising |
| DIO | Matching demand | Building |
| DPO | Stable or extending | Shortening |
| CCC | Declining | Rising |

## Free Cash Flow Analysis

### FCF Calculation

| Method | Calculation |
|--------|-------------|
| Standard | OCF - CapEx |
| Levered | OCF - CapEx - Interest |
| Unlevered | OCF - CapEx + Interest(1-Tax) |

### FCF Quality Assessment

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Consistency | Stable or growing |
| Margin | FCF / Revenue trend |
| Coverage | FCF / Debt service |
| Return | FCF / Equity value |

### FCF Drivers

| Driver | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Revenue Growth | Positive if profitable |
| Margin Expansion | Positive |
| Working Capital | Efficiency gains |
| CapEx Reduction | Positive (if sustainable) |

## Red Flags Summary

### Operating Red Flags

1. **OCF < Net Income** - Earnings quality issue
2. **Negative OCF** - Cash burning
3. **Declining OCF** - Deteriorating operations
4. **Volatile OCF** - Instability

### Working Capital Red Flags

1. **Rising receivables** - Collection problems
2. **Inventory buildup** - Demand issues
3. **Declining payables** - Supplier pressure
4. **Extending CCC** - Efficiency decline

### Investing Red Flags

1. **CapEx declining** - Underinvestment
2. **Heavy acquisitions** - Integration risk
3. **Asset sales** - Cash desperation
4. **Investment losses** - Poor allocation

### Financing Red Flags

1. **Heavy debt issuance** - Leverage risk
2. **Dividend suspension** - Cash stress
3. **Forced equity** - Distress raise
4. **Covenant violations** - Credit issues
